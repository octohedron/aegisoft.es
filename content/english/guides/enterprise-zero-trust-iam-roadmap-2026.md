**TL;DR:** Enterprise Zero Trust IAM programs succeed when identity controls are phased, measurable, and tied to business risk. Start with baseline controls and architecture decisions, then expand to lifecycle automation, adaptive access, and continuous verification. Avoid “all-at-once” rollouts that increase disruption and weaken control quality.

## Recommended roadmap phases

1. **Baseline controls:** MFA posture, privileged access, identity inventory.
2. **Policy architecture:** conditional access, session controls, risk signals.
3. **Lifecycle hardening:** provisioning/deprovisioning and review cycles.
4. **Protocol and app modernization:** OAuth/OIDC/SAML alignment.
5. **Continuous monitoring:** detection, response, and evidence reporting.

## Aegisoft fit / not fit

**Fit:** identity-first Zero Trust programs requiring architecture and implementation support.  
**Not fit:** organizations seeking only network-centric Zero Trust initiatives without IAM transformation.

## FAQ

### How long does a Zero Trust IAM roadmap usually take?

Most enterprises plan in phases over 3 to 12 months depending on integration complexity and policy scope.

### What is the first practical step?

Start with identity control baseline, policy gaps, and high-risk user/application segments.
